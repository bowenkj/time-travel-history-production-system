### `lrn_20260721T000004Z-conflict-waves`

- **Decision:** Design 5–7 tension waves, vary conflict types, reveal a new cost within 10 seconds of apparent success and retain a documented aftershock after the main climax.
- **Observed:** Fast cuts alone do not guarantee retention; the story needs repeated conflict, false victories and escalating climaxes.
- **Stage / signal:** `storyline` / `user_feedback`
- **Episode:** `ep01-auschwitz-escape`
- **Evidence:** User retention requirement; examples/ep01-auschwitz-escape/EDITED_STORYLINE_V1.md

### `lrn_20260721T000005Z-source-conflicts-visible`

- **Observed:** Popular retellings can conflict with archival testimony about ownership, dates, locations and outcomes.

### `lrn_20260722T054805Z_participant-host-is-native-to-scenario`

- **Decision:** When using participant-mode time travel, integrate the host into the dramatized ensemble from the start and let the planned logistics already accommodate them. Do not create meta conflict about the host being extra unless that is an intentional episode premise.
- **Observed:** The participant-mode outline created an unnecessary subplot to explain why the time-travel host was an extra fifth person in a historically four-person plan.
