# Publication and Packaging

## Alignment rule

The title, thumbnail and first 30 seconds must express the same central conflict. A
strong package earns a click that the opening immediately repays.

## Title QA

A strong title usually contains:

- a recognizable person/place/object;
- an action or attempt;
- a reversal, consequence, discovery or unresolved result;
- plain spoken English;
- the strongest idea before mobile truncation.

Explore multiple modes rather than forcing one formula:

- action → reversal;
- denial → evidence;
- routine situation → impossible discovery;
- challenge/question;
- justice/karma emotion;
- identity or relationship revelation.

Do not add names that the audience does not know unless the identity itself creates
the hook. Avoid punctuation habits that weaken natural language.

## Thumbnail QA

- one immediately readable human emotion or action;
- one recognizable historical object/place;
- clear foreground/background hierarchy;
- consistent channel logo placement, scale and margins;
- no graphic suffering as click bait;
- text only when it adds information not already carried by the title/image;
- remove irrelevant camera codes or clutter when ethically and technically permitted;
- test two or three genuinely different hypotheses, not minor color variants.

## Description

Use the first two lines for the conflict and curiosity gap, not a subscribe link.
Then include episode summary, source/disclosure notes, chapters, CTA and sources.

## Upload checklist

- correct master and thumbnail versions;
- title/thumbnail test configured as intended;
- description and chapters;
- captions/subtitles;
- audience and category;
- synthetic/altered-content disclosure when required;
- graphic-content, age, copyright and restrictions review;
- end screen, cards and playlist;
- visibility and publish timing;
- final watch on the uploaded transcode.

## Performance review

Capture 24-hour, 7-day and 28-day snapshots when available. Interpret:

- impressions before CTR;
- CTR by traffic source;
- first-30-second retention;
- average view duration and percentage viewed;
- title/thumbnail test watch-time share;
- comments that reveal confusion or emotional payoff.

Record reusable evidence through the self-learning loop.
