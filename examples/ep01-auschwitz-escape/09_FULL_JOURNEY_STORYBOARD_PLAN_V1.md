## Why the hook is deferred

The edited storyline still needs a consequence-first cold open, but that does not mean
the cold open should be storyboarded or generated first. Its best material will come
from complete conflict beats inside the journey—most likely the failed weapons-room
key, Bendera's exposed garage walk, the unmoving final barrier, the gate order, or the
post-escape search. Storyboard and later generate those beats in their real context
first. Then select a hook that is visually strong, understandable, and ends at a
deliberate question without cutting a sentence or action arbitrarily.

Existing hook boards in `storyboard/v1/` are exploratory only.

## Storyboard constraints

- Black graphite and grey marker only; blank or minimally indicated faces.
- No final likeness, insignia, logo, host casting or production lighting.
- Preserve Bendera/Piechowski/Jaster/Lempart silhouette and seat locks.
- Preserve inner-gate versus outer-checkpoint distinction.
- Preserve left-to-right movement toward and through final barrier.
- No invented chase, violence, gunfire, dogs or mechanical explosion.
- No fabricated death list, pass, quote or exact car-failure cause.
- Every panel must change information, obstacle, choice, location, proof or risk.

## Hook selection gate — later

After journey storyboards and journey footage exist, score candidate hook beats on:

1. visual clarity without prior exposition;
2. complete conflict exchange rather than random highlights;
3. emotional or procedural stakes inside five seconds;
4. ability to cut before resolution without truncating speech unnaturally;
5. faithful payoff later in the episode;
6. production quality and character continuity of the actual available footage.

The final hook may use one beat or a short progression of beats, but should not require
new fictional footage made only to look exciting.
