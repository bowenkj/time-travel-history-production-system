- **Host:** explains geography and invisible constraints but never supplies a solution

## Cold-reader comprehension

A viewer should be able to answer, without prior knowledge:

1. **What are they trying to do?** Cross the outer checkpoint in a stolen SS car.
2. **Why can they not simply drive out?** They remain inside a wider guarded zone and
   lack valid papers.
3. **Why these four men?** Each controls a different necessary permission or skill.
4. **What nearly ruins the plan?** A failed weapons-room key and a final barrier that
   does not open.
5. **Why remain after the gate opens?** The search starts, the car fails, and Jaster's
   hidden report is revealed.

Decision: **PASS.**

## Highest remaining risks

1. **Mission-brief delivery speed:** must be tested with the eventual host voice.
2. **Four-face continuity:** the audience must instantly distinguish all four men in
   the car and warehouse.
3. **Security geography:** the inner gate and outer checkpoint must never look like the
   same location.
4. **Tone:** suspense must come from the documented mechanism, not from action-movie
   exaggeration or triumphant treatment of the camp setting.
5. **Reconstruction labeling:** realistic synthetic scenes require a consistent visual
   disclosure system.
