# Full Journey Storyboard Plan V2 — Host-Led Time-Travel Grammar

Status: **generation blueprint**  
Episode: **I Escaped Auschwitz With Four Prisoners**  
Format: nine monochrome 3×3 storyboard sheets, chronological journey first  
Source locks: `04_SCRIPT_V2.md` + `06_CONTINUITY_BIBLE_V2.md`

## Global drawing contract

- Low-cost black graphite and grey marker storyboard, not finished concept art.
- Every panel is 16:9 and carries only its panel number; no readable captions or UI.
- The fictional host is the same lean, female-presenting figure with dark hair tightly
  pulled back throughout. Her compact matte-black camera is a recurring silhouette.
- The plan always contains five people. Never stage confusion about a fifth member.
- Bendera is broad and mechanic-built; Piechowski compact; Jaster tallest; Lempart
  narrow; host average-height and visually distinct through camera posture.
- At least five panels per historical sheet use host selfie, host POV, or direct host
  interaction. No three detached omniscient panels in a row.
- Do not depict generic atrocity imagery, invented pursuit, gunfire, dogs, portals,
  modern phones, readable Nazi insignia, or heroic uniform glamour.
- Outer checkpoint stays road-right; the Steyr moves left-to-right through it.

## Storyboard acceptance gates

1. Each historical sheet has at least five embodied host panels.
2. The host remains the same silhouette through all wardrobe states.
3. Every group frame contains exactly five participants unless Bendera is deliberately
   walking alone or the host is explicitly shooting POV.
4. Sheets 03–06 preserve a continuous path; nobody teleports into HWL or the car.
5. Sheet 07 preserves seating, right-side guard booth and left-to-right crossing.
6. The final barrier exchange is readable even with all captions removed.
7. No panel requires an arbitrary metaphor to explain the spoken beat.
8. The complete board reads as one woman experiencing the escape with four men—not a
   conventional reenactment with occasional presenter inserts.

## Hook policy

Do not generate a separate hook sheet yet. After the chronological journey passes QA,
derive the cold open from panels 705–708, ending on Piechowski opening the door before
his command.
