# Continuity Bible V2 — EP01 Auschwitz Escape

Status: **paper lock for host-led storyboard V2**  
Date: 2026-07-21  
Scope: participant-mode host, four historical prisoners, five-person formation, Steyr
seating, selfie camera grammar, HWL route and two-gate geography  

## Continuity principle

The host is the fifth member of the dramatized escape plan from the beginning. Do not
create visual or dialogue beats questioning why there are five people. The work detail,
warehouse movement, uniforms and vehicle blocking already accommodate all five.

The four historical men retain the actions documented in the source record. The host
participates, reacts and records; the host does not take Bendera's planning, keys or
driving, Piechowski's German or barrier order, Jaster's lock opportunity or report, or
Lempart's decisive prompt.

## Host lock

### Provisional identity for storyboard

- Use one consistent female-presenting host stand-in because V2 script pronouns and
  channel reference grammar currently assume a woman on camera.
- Exact face, name, ethnicity, voice and final hair remain pending brand/casting lock.
- Storyboard host: average height, lean frame, oval face, dark hair pulled tightly back
  or completely contained under historical headwear.
- This stand-in is a blocking identity, not an approved production likeness.

### Host behavior

- Direct eye contact with the viewer in selfie mode.
- Looks away from the lens when immediate danger or another person demands attention.
- Speaks in complete but short field sentences; under pressure, silence and breathing
  replace explanation.
- Moves with the group instead of presenting beside it.
- Participates in routine labor: carries ash, pushes trolley, climbs through hatch,
  changes clothing, enters car and walks forest route.
- Does not give historical participants advice that causes their documented choices.
- Does not celebrate at the barrier. Relief appears only after the entire car clears.

### Camera convention

- The compact handheld/selfie camera is a channel time-travel convention. Historical
  characters and guards do not comment on it.
- It must remain the same small matte-black device; no phone screen, modern logo,
  gimbal, microphone cage or visible interface.
- The host normally holds it in the right hand. If continuity requires a free hand for
  the trolley, hatch or climb, it becomes a tight chest-mounted POV without explaining
  the switch.
- Do not let the device become a plot obstacle or a source of light in dark interiors.
- Selfie lens remains wide but not fisheye-distorted. Keep faces and period geometry
  credible.

## Five-person blocking locks

### F1 — Planning table

Clockwise from camera-left foreground:

1. Host at foreground-left;
2. Piechowski at table head / speaking position;
3. Jaster at rear-center, tallest even when leaning;
4. Bendera at camera-right beside key/mechanical objects;
5. Lempart between host and Jaster beside the five-position tally.

### F2 — Rubbish detail

- Piechowski leads and reports the group.
- Host pushes front-right side of trolley and owns selfie/POV.
- Bendera pushes rear-left.
- Jaster remains rear-center and visibly tallest.
- Lempart pushes rear-right.
- Five striped bodies and one trolley must read in a single wide shot before the inner
  gate.

### F3 — Warehouse movement

- Piechowski leads toward official contact points.
- Bendera handles keys and forced door.
- Jaster monitors lock route and report.
- Lempart holds rear security.
- Host remains inside the formation, normally second-to-last, and supplies POV without
  becoming scout or leader.

### F4 — Steyr seating

Treat the open-top car as having a two-seat front and a rear bench that can hold three:

- front-left: Bendera, driver;
- front-right: Piechowski, senior-looking speaking officer;
- rear-left: Jaster, tallest, knees angled slightly inward;
- rear-center: host, camera kept low until selfie beats;
- rear-right: Lempart, directly behind Piechowski and close enough to prompt him.

Never swap the host and Lempart. Never place Piechowski at the wheel. Never allow the
host to block the visual line between Lempart and Piechowski during the final stop.

## Location and geography locks

### G1 — Modern briefing space

- Compact dark-neutral YouTuber/podcast environment, not a news desk.
- Host framed medium-close with physical/animated route beside her.
- Diagram always reads left-to-right:
  `INNER PRISONER COMPOUND → HWL / GARAGE → OUTER CHECKPOINT`.
- Five objects appear only when named: work-detail tally, improvised key, uniform,
  Steyr key/car, barrier.
- No generic Holocaust montage behind the host.

### G2 — Inner prisoner compound and gate

- Dense brick blocks, ordered paths, prisoner gate, guard oversight.
- Inner gate is architectural and embedded in compound.
- Five-person rubbish detail crosses on foot with trolley.
- Guard ledger sits camera-right of the movement lane and remains on that side.
- Never reuse outer-checkpoint booth/barrier here.

### G3 — Wider guarded support zone

- Open industrial ground between inner compound and HWL/garage.
- Sight line must reveal tower/guard positions, service paths, warehouse, garage and
  distant outer checkpoint as different layers.
- Host's first historical selfie rotates through this geography instead of presenting
  a detached aerial map.

### G4 — HWL / SS repository

- Closed industrial repository with prepared coal hatch, dim basement, internal
  corridor/stairs, secured uniform/weapons room and side exit toward garage.
- Route remains: exterior approach → hatch down → basement → key-opened doors → failed
  weapons-room key → crowbar → change → side entrance.
- Forced frame damage persists after the door opens.
- Host passes through every space with the group; no teleporting between rooms.

### G5 — Garage and exposed yard

- Separate low industrial garage within clear open walking distance of HWL.
- Bendera crosses alone. Host, Piechowski, Jaster and Lempart remain concealed at side
  entrance and observe from one stable axis.
- Garage door, lock, car bay and exit direction remain fixed.

### G6 — Outer checkpoint

- Small booth, horizontal hand-operated barrier and long straight approach.
- Booth, guard and crank remain road-right; open road lies beyond.
- Steyr approaches and exits left-to-right in all external coverage.
- Host's rear-center selfie may reverse background motion inside the car, but it must
  not flip the physical booth/barrier geography.
- Only one guard controls the barrier and receives Piechowski's order.

### G7 — Forest route and separation

- Daylight forest roads, never a night chase.
- Steyr becomes unusable without showing a precise failed component.
- Group leaves car together before paths separate.
- Host returns to modern debrief by a hard editorial cut, not an on-screen portal.

## Hero props and vehicle

### P1 — Steyr 220

- Black Austrian Steyr 220 open-top cabriolet/staff car; long bonnet, separate fenders,
  period headlamps and consistent four-door/open-rear-bench geometry.
- Escape telegram records registration **SS-20 898**; keep plate stable when legible.
- Left-hand drive unless stronger vehicle evidence proves otherwise.
- Fixed five-person seating follows F4.
- Used staff vehicle, not showroom-polished.
- No bullet holes, chase damage, crash or dramatic mechanical explosion.
- Final failure remains mechanically ambiguous.

### P2 — Rubbish trolley

- Heavy five-person work trolley with ash and refuse; one uneven wheel supplies audio
  motif.
- Same shape, handle height and wheel defect from kitchen to inner gate.
- Abandoned before warehouse entry.

### P3 — Improvised keys

- Small workmanlike keys; no modern lock picks or ornate skeleton-key design.
- One identifiable ring with stable count/order.
- Bendera handles the keys; host may demonstrate a separate replica only in the modern
  or clearly explanatory insert.

### P4 — Crowbar and damaged door

- Plain period iron crowbar.
- Bendera initiates force; collective group may brace, but host does not replace him.
- Pry marks and split wood persist.

### P5 — Jaster's report

- Small folded packet concealed beneath clothing.
- No modern envelope, title page, wax seal or readable expositional headline.
- Never give it to host before the forest reveal.
- Host notices the protective gesture but does not know content until Jaster reveals it.

### P6 — Ledger and escape telegram

- Inner ledger is handwritten and local to first gate.
- Escape telegram is later and visually distinct.
- Participant-mode story may show five missing positions, but any explicitly labeled
  archival record must retain the historical four names. Do not create a fake archival
  document naming the host.

### P7 — Host camera

- Single compact matte-black time-travel camera.
- No brand, on-screen UI, modern audio meters or glowing screen visible to viewer.
- Stable right-hand grip orientation in selfie shots.
- Camera may disappear from external angles only when the shot is explicitly the
  device's POV.

## Date, weather and lighting

- Saturday, 20 June 1942.
- Early summer in southern occupied Poland.
- Escape begins after lunch; vehicle departure approximately 3:45 p.m.
- Restrained late-afternoon daylight with soft-to-moderate cloud cover.
- No rain, snow, sunset, night driving or dramatic storm.
- HWL interior is cool and dim but faces, keys and door state remain readable.
- Final gate occurs in full afternoon visibility; danger comes from being seen.

## Visual grammar locks

### Modern mode

- Stable 35–50 mm-equivalent medium/medium-close framing.
- Clean composition; tactile route objects; restrained graphics.
- Host speaks confidently and knows the source record.

### Historical participant mode

- 20–28 mm-equivalent handheld selfie and over-shoulder POV.
- Populated, moving background; host does not stand in an empty set to narrate.
- Camera height and arm length remain consistent.
- Host talks from current uncertainty even when modern audience knows the outcome.
- At least half of non-climax storyboard panels must contain host, host POV or direct
  host interaction with environment.
- No three consecutive detached omniscient frames.

### Historical action mode

- 35–75 mm-equivalent restrained cinematic coverage when the four men's documented
  actions need spatial clarity.
- Always return to host experience at sequence transition.
- Do not use detached wide shots merely because they are visually attractive.

### Evidence mode

- Archive/evidence is labeled and visually distinct from reconstructed footage.
- No fake archival image or document includes the fictional host.
- Do not use unrelated atrocity imagery as emotional wallpaper.

### Final-barrier climax

- Preserve left-to-right movement and F4 seating.
- Host camera remains rear-center and low enough to keep Piechowski/Lempart readable.
- Once car stops, host says nothing until rear clears barrier.
- Reduce cut frequency; show guard decision, crank movement and full physical crossing.

## Audio continuity

- Host camera perspective uses close dry voice with environment behind it.
- External historical coverage widens ambience without changing location acoustics.
- Recurring motifs: trolley wheel, metal key, Steyr idle, barrier crank.
- Near-silence: ledger decision, failed key, garage glance, stopped car.
- Do not run maximum-intensity music beneath routine labor or explanation.
- Strongest release cue begins only after full car clears, then becomes search pulse.

## Known generation risks

1. Losing the host between historical shots and reverting to detached documentary.
2. Host face/hair/wardrobe drift across modern, prisoner and SS states.
3. Female-presenting host disguise becoming a separate unplanned story problem.
4. Five-person group collapsing back to four or growing to six.
5. Rear-seat swaps among Jaster, host and Lempart.
6. Host blocking Lempart's line to Piechowski during the final prompt.
7. Face drift among four shaved Polish men.
8. Losing Jaster's 193 cm silhouette.
9. Mirrored steering wheel, booth, crank, holster or road direction.
10. Generic sedan replacing the open-top Steyr.
11. Generic prison fence replacing two-layer geography.
12. Modern hardware, camera UI or logos entering historical frame.
13. SS uniform inaccuracies or glamour treatment.
14. Invented chase, gunfire, dog attack or mechanical explosion.
15. Fake archival material that places the host in the historical record.

## Storyboard proof gates

Before any production-quality generation, the black-and-white V2 storyboard must prove:

1. one consistent host silhouette across modern, prisoner, SS and forest states;
2. one readable five-person planning-table frame;
3. one readable five-person rubbish-detail frame;
4. host-led selfie/POV grammar in every major transition;
5. complete warehouse route with no host teleportation;
6. Bendera's solo garage crossing observed from the fixed side entrance;
7. five-person Steyr seating in exterior, interior and selfie views;
8. Lempart remains directly behind Piechowski and can prompt him;
9. final barrier geography never flips;
10. host remains silent during complete stopped-car exchange;
11. archival/evidence frames never contain the fictional host;
12. the full journey reads as a time-travel vlog rather than a reenactment interrupted
    by occasional explanation.
