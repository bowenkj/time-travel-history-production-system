# EP01 Script V1 — Four Prisoners, One Car, No Papers

Status: production paper script  
Target runtime: 10:45–11:15  
Draft date: 2026-07-21  
Format: host-led immersive history / realistic reconstruction  

## Editorial contract

- The host is a field correspondent, never a fictional fifth escapee.
- No portal, countdown, or visible time-travel sequence. The mission briefing hard-cuts
  into documented action.
- Dialogue not preserved in testimony is avoided. The decisive gate order is presented
  as a translated reconstruction because published English versions differ.
- Reconstructed historical scenes must be disclosed as altered or synthetic. Archive
  material, when used, must be labeled separately.
- Do not identify the Steyr as Rudolf Höss's car. The Auschwitz Museum's lesson names
  HWL chief SS-Hauptsturmführer Paul Kreutzmann as its user.

# OPENING PROOF GATE — 0:00–1:30

## 0:00–0:15 — Cold open: the barrier does not move

**STORY FUNCTION**  
Show the terminal problem before explaining the plan. Create a clean unanswered
question and cut before the answer.

**VISUAL**  
Extreme close-up: a Steyr 220 engine vibrates. Gravel moves beneath its tires. Four
men sit inside wearing SS uniforms. None looks at the others. Bendera's fingers tighten
on the steering wheel. The outer barrier grows in the windshield: 80 metres, 60, 20.
The guard watches, but his hand does not touch the control. The car stops. Jaster keeps
one hand against the folded paper hidden beneath his coat. Piechowski's hand hovers
near his holster. Lempart leans forward from the rear seat.

**NARRATION**  
> Four prisoners. A stolen SS car. Stolen uniforms. And no papers that could survive
> a single question.

**DIALOGUE**  
> LEMPART: Kazek... do something.

Piechowski opens the door. Cut before he speaks.

**AUDIO**  
Low engine, loose gravel, one narrowing pulse. Music drops almost completely when the
car stops. Lempart's line is dry and close. Door latch becomes the cut sound.

**SOURCE / RECONSTRUCTION NOTE**  
The approach distances and Lempart's prompt derive from Piechowski's later testimony.
The precise blocking and facial reactions are restrained reconstruction.

**EXIT HOOK**  
How did four prisoners get this far—and what can Piechowski possibly say now?

## 0:15–0:21 — Brand ident

**STORY FUNCTION**  
Six-second recognition beat without releasing the tension.

**VISUAL**  
`[CHANNEL LOGO]` over a rapid evidence montage: a damaged lock, a rubbish trolley, SS
insignia, a car key, and the barrier handle. Use the approved brand animation once the
channel identity is finalized.

**HOST / NARRATION**  
None.

**AUDIO**  
Continue the cold-open pulse. One mechanical lock-strike lands on the logo. The last
sound carries directly into the host's first word.

---

## 0:21–0:48 — Mission briefing

**STORY FUNCTION**  
Give the viewer the date, place, objective, and security geometry in under 30 seconds.

**VISUAL**  
HOST direct to camera. Behind the host, a three-point diagram builds in motion:
PRISONER COMPOUND → SS WAREHOUSE / GARAGE → OUTER CHECKPOINT. A key, uniform, car,
and barrier appear only when named.

**HOST**  
> Auschwitz. June 20, 1942. Getting beyond the prisoners' gate would not make you
> free. A wider SS guard line still surrounded the warehouses, the garage, and every
> road out.
>
> So four prisoners would have to leave as a legal work detail, break into an SS
> storehouse, become the officers guarding them, steal a staff car—and make the final
> guard open the barrier for them.
>
> To see how they ended up inside that car, we begin with a mechanic who had only half
> a plan.

**TRANSITION**  
Hard cut on “half a plan” to Bendera's hands working beneath the Steyr's open hood.
No travel effect.

**AUDIO**  
Restrained ticking texture beneath the diagram. A short ignition sound motivates the
hard cut.

**EXIT HOOK**  
Bendera can obtain the car. The harder problem is making the camp release it.

---

## 0:48–1:29 — Half a plan

**STORY FUNCTION**  
Introduce Bendera through his capability, add urgency, then turn escape into a moral
and procedural problem for Piechowski.

**VISUAL**  
1942 reconstruction. Bendera repairs the Austrian Steyr 220 inside the SS garage.
HOST stands outside the work lane, observing without entering the action. Cut to a
restrained reconstruction of Bendera finding Piechowski. Then visualize the camp as
two boundaries with the car trapped between them. Show other prisoners returning to
their real work groups at roll call.

**HOST / NARRATION**  
> Eugeniusz Bendera was a prisoner mechanic. He knew the garage, he knew the cars, and
> he had already chosen this one.
>
> Years later, Kazimierz Piechowski recalled that Bendera had learned he was marked to
> be killed. He needed out. He believed he could get them a car.
>
> But Piechowski saw two problems. A prisoner at the wheel would never cross the outer
> checkpoint. And if they vanished from a real work group, other prisoners could be
> punished for losing them.
>
> Their answer was not to break the security system. It was to give every guard exactly
> what he expected to see.

**VISUAL PAYOFF**  
Four empty places appear around a rough worktable.

Do not fabricate or display a legible “death list” or order. Keep the warning attached
to Piechowski's attributed recollection through a small source tag.

**EXIT HOOK**  
> First, they needed a work detail that did not exist.

# MAIN STORY

## 1:29–2:10 — Build a team that does not exist

**STORY FUNCTION**  
Introduce the other three men only through the job each must perform. Plant Jaster's
hidden payload without explaining it.

**VISUAL**  
Four men arrive separately. Each contribution appears as action rather than a profile:
Piechowski listens to an SS order in German and repeats it accurately; Jaster studies
a lock; Lempart counts four places beside a rubbish trolley; Bendera sets down the car
key. Jaster receives a folded report and hides it under his clothing, seen only in a
brief insert.

**NARRATION**  
> Piechowski spoke German and could report the group. Stanisław Jaster belonged to the
> camp resistance and could help create access. Józef Lempart made them four—the size
> of a believable rubbish detail. Bendera knew the machine that could carry them out.
>
> Four prisoners. Four separate permissions. All of them had to arrive at the same
> place on the same afternoon.

**VISUAL**  
The four objects land in sequence: tally, lock, insignia, car key.

**NARRATION**  
> There was one immediate problem. The uniforms and weapons that would transform them
> were locked inside an SS building.

**EXIT HOOK**  
The only legal way to touch its locks is to be ordered to repair them.

---

## 2:10–3:00 — Break the locks to make the keys

**STORY FUNCTION**  
Turn preparation into a causal mini-heist, then foreshadow the door that will resist
the quiet plan.

**VISUAL**  
Jaster discreetly damages three lock mechanisms. An SS supervisor discovers the
faults and brings Bendera over. Bendera repairs them under observation, preserving the
shapes he needs. At night, improvised keys take form. In parallel, Piechowski loosens
the coal-hatch bolt and works rank tabs onto a uniform piece.

**HOST**  
Beside an enlarged lock mechanism:

> Jaster creates the fault. The SS assigns Bendera to fix it. And the repair gives
> Bendera what the prisoners could never steal openly: access to the lock itself.

**NARRATION**  
> Bendera later said he made bump keys for the route through the building. Piechowski
> prepared the coal hatch and the insignia that would make one stolen uniform look
> senior to the others.

**AUDIO**  
Tactile metal clicks replace music for several seconds. Three successful lock turns,
then a fourth turn that stops halfway.

**NARRATION**  
> They now had a quiet way through most of the storehouse.

**EXIT HOOK**  
> Most would not be enough.

---

## 3:00–3:48 — Saturday: the plan becomes irreversible

**STORY FUNCTION**  
Move from preparation to action, with no farewell monologue.

**VISUAL**  
Saturday work groups disperse after midday. The four wait in the attic of an unfinished
block. Light cuts through wooden slats. Four hands check a crude route. A brief silent
beat of prayer. Behind the kitchen, ash and rubbish strike a trolley. The wheels begin
turning toward the prisoner gate.

**NARRATION**  
> They chose Saturday, when work ended early and the SS offices around the warehouse
> would be thinly staffed. Piechowski later recalled that the four met in an attic,
> reviewed the route, prayed for their families, and agreed they could not be captured.
>
> After lunch, they walked behind the kitchen, filled a trolley with ash and rubbish,
> and became the work detail they had invented.

**AUDIO**  
Work bell, distant boots leaving, then the uneven trolley wheel becomes the rhythm.

**VISUAL**  
At the gate, the guard looks up from his ledger.

**EXIT HOOK**  
> Now the fake detail had to survive its first official record.

---

## 3:48–4:34 — The first gate

**STORY FUNCTION**  
Deliver the first false victory, then immediately reveal that the apparent escape is
only entry into a larger guarded zone.

**VISUAL**  
Piechowski stands at the front of the trolley. The guard waits. Close-up of pencil and
ledger.

**NARRATION**  
> Piechowski gave his prisoner number—and added one word: “three.” Three prisoners
> under his supervision.

The guard writes. The gate opens. The trolley crosses.

**AUDIO**  
No music until the wheels clear the threshold. Give the viewer one breath of relief.

**HOST**  
As the camera pulls wide and reveals the support zone, towers, and distant barrier:

> This looks like freedom. It is not. They have left the inner compound, but they are
> still inside Auschwitz's wider guard chain. The SS warehouse is ahead. The final
> checkpoint is beyond it.

**VISUAL**  
The four approach the closed HWL building with nobody else in sight.

**EXIT HOOK**  
> And if anyone sees a rubbish detail entering a locked building after work, the cover
> is over.

---

## 4:34–5:29 — The key that fails

**STORY FUNCTION**  
Pay off the access plan, break it at the most dangerous door, and convert stealth into
physical evidence.

**VISUAL**  
The prepared coal hatch opens into darkness. The men slip into the basement. Coal dust
marks hands and clothing. Bendera's keys open one door, then another. At the weapons
room, the key enters but will not turn.

**AUDIO**  
Music disappears. Key scrape. Breath. A second attempt. Silence.

**NARRATION**  
> The copied keys carry them through the route—until the one door that matters most.

**VISUAL**  
A crowbar appears. The four exchange one look. Metal bites into the frame. The first
attempt slips. The second bends the wood. On the third, the door cracks open.

**AUDIO**  
Make the break uncomfortably loud. Hold a beat afterward as if the entire compound
heard it.

**HOST / NARRATION**  
> Even if the four men escape, that forced door will soon tell the SS exactly what
> happened.

**VISUAL**  
Uniforms and weapons wait behind the broken frame.

**EXIT HOOK**  
> They can finally become SS officers—except for the one man who must still look like
> a prisoner.

---

## 5:29–6:20 — Three officers and one prisoner

**STORY FUNCTION**  
Create an identity paradox and isolate Bendera for an exposed crossing.

**VISUAL**  
Piechowski, Jaster, and Lempart change. Focus on function: rank tab, belt, helmet,
weapon. Piechowski wears the highest rank. Bendera puts on SS underclothes, then pulls
his striped prisoner blouse back over them. Jaster checks that the folded report is
still concealed.

**NARRATION**  
> Piechowski, Jaster, and Lempart dressed first. Piechowski took the senior uniform—the
> role that would have to speak if anyone challenged them.
>
> Bendera did the opposite. At the garage, guards knew him as a prisoner mechanic who
> sometimes moved SS vehicles. If he crossed the yard already dressed as an officer,
> the disguise could expose him. So he hid it beneath the identity the guards expected.

**VISUAL**  
The side door opens onto a long, exposed route to the garage. Bendera steps out alone.

**AUDIO**  
Near silence. Distant compound ambience. Each footstep distinct.

**EXIT HOOK**  
There is no cover story if a guard asks why this prisoner is opening the SS garage.

---

## 6:20–7:12 — Steal the car in plain sight

**STORY FUNCTION**  
Deliver the vehicle payoff through routine rather than spectacle; assemble all four
dependencies in one car.

**VISUAL**  
Bendera crosses the open yard. A guard glances over and looks away. Bendera unlocks
the garage. Inside waits the Steyr 220 he had repaired. Key in ignition. Engine turns,
hesitates, then starts. He drives it openly back to the warehouse side entrance.

**NARRATION**  
> No alarm. Bendera had moved cars before on the orders of the garage staff. What looks
> to us like theft looks, for these few seconds, like another prisoner's assignment.

**HOST**  
Brief field-correspondent insert beside the moving car:

> This was the staff car used by HWL chief Paul Kreutzmann—not, as the story is often
> retold, the camp commandant's personal car.

**VISUAL**  
Bendera returns, removes the prisoner blouse, completes the uniform, and takes the
driver's seat. The others load in. Insert of the main clock: approximately 3:45 p.m.

**NARRATION**  
> At about a quarter to four, the keys, uniforms, weapons, driver, and bluff were finally
> inside the same machine.

**EXIT HOOK**  
> Then SS men began saluting them.

---

## 7:12–7:57 — The disguise works too well

**STORY FUNCTION**  
Give a dangerous false victory that tightens rather than releases tension.

**VISUAL**  
The Steyr rolls through the support area. One SS man salutes. Piechowski acknowledges
him without enthusiasm. Another salute. Bendera keeps a measured speed. Inside the
car, nobody celebrates. Jaster grips the hidden report. Lempart watches the guard booth
growing larger.

**NARRATION**  
> The uniform does what the prisoners hoped. It tells every SS man who sees the car not
> to question the people inside it.
>
> But the salutes prove only that the disguise works at a distance. There is still no
> evidence the four possess the pass required at the outer guard line.

**AUDIO**  
Music gains forward motion but holds one unresolved low note. Engine rhythm becomes a
countdown.

**VISUAL**  
The barrier fills the windshield. The guard remains still.

**NARRATION**  
> And the barrier does not move.

**TRANSITION**  
Match directly into the cold-open framing. Do not repeat the initial orientation.

---

## 7:57–9:16 — “Kazek, do something”

**STORY FUNCTION**  
The physical climax. Preparation runs out; Lempart, Piechowski, and Bendera must create
the final permission in real time.

**VISUAL**  
Distance compresses: 80 metres. The guard looks at the car. 60. No movement at the
barrier. 20. Bendera's face drains. His foot touches the brake. The Steyr stops.
Piechowski stares through the windshield. No one speaks for one full beat.

**AUDIO**  
Strip out the score. Engine idle only.

**DIALOGUE**  
> LEMPART: Kazek, do something.

**VISUAL**  
Piechowski opens the door, steps one foot out, and becomes the senior officer his
uniform claims he is. His hand moves toward the holster.

**DIALOGUE — TRANSLATED RECONSTRUCTION**  
> PIECHOWSKI: Are you asleep? Open this gate! Now!

**VISUAL**  
The guard snaps into motion. He reaches the handle, raises the barrier, and salutes.
Piechowski holds the stare for one fraction longer, gets back in, and shuts the door.
Bendera drives. Keep the shot until the full rear of the car clears the barrier.

**AUDIO**  
Piechowski's order is dry and foregrounded. Barrier mechanism groans. The score returns
only when the car is completely outside.

**NARRATION**  
After the crossing, and not before:

> The gate opens because the guard sees rank, anger, and certainty—and obeys before he
> asks for proof.

**EXIT HOOK**  
> But the four men are now missing, the car is unmistakable, and the camp has not yet
> counted its prisoners.

---

## 9:16–10:04 — The escape creates a manhunt

**STORY FUNCTION**  
Prevent the gate from feeling like the ending. Crosscut freedom with a documented
pursuit clock and remove the car advantage.

**VISUAL**  
The Steyr leaves the main road for forest routes. Intercut: evening roll call; four
empty positions; an officer checks the count; a duty note is written; an emergency SS
search unit mobilizes; an escape telegram carrying four names begins moving toward
police and border authorities.

**NARRATION**  
> At roll call, the camp finds four men missing. An emergency search unit is sent out.
> A telegram begins carrying their names to police and border posts.
>
> Outside the barrier, the Steyr buys distance—but it cannot make them disappear.

**VISUAL**  
The car slows on a forest route and eventually stops. Do not show a precise mechanical
cause. The four leave it, cover or discard identifying uniform pieces, and continue on
foot. Their paths divide.

**HOST**  
Beside the abandoned car:

> Surviving accounts disagree on exactly where the car's journey ended. They agree on
> what mattered: from here, the four had to continue without it.

**AUDIO**  
Pursuit pulse under the roll-call crosscut. Drop to forest ambience at the stopped car.

**EXIT HOOK**  
> And one of them was carrying something the SS could not be allowed to recover.

---

## 10:04–10:52 — The hidden second mission

**STORY FUNCTION**  
Reveal the report as a second payoff, then resolve the four lives without turning the
ending into four biographies.

**VISUAL**  
Jaster removes the folded report. Transition to a moving map: his route toward Warsaw
and the Home Army; the other three paths splitting away. The official escape telegram
travels outward in the opposite direction.

**NARRATION**  
> Beneath Jaster's stolen uniform was a report prepared by resistance organizer Witold
> Pilecki—evidence from inside Auschwitz intended for Home Army headquarters. The
> Auschwitz Museum says Jaster probably completed that mission.
>
> Lempart hid near the region his family came from and survived the war. Bendera lived
> under false papers and survived through his work as a mechanic. Piechowski joined the
> Home Army and survived.
>
> Jaster entered Home Army special operations. He died in Warsaw in 1943 in circumstances
> the Museum describes as unresolved. His parents were later imprisoned at Auschwitz.
> Both died there.

**VISUAL**  
End on the report moving beyond the map boundary, not on a triumphant group pose.

**NARRATION**  
> The car carried four prisoners beyond the guard line. Jaster carried testimony with
> them.

**EXIT HOOK**  
Resolve the mechanism of the escape in one final thought.

---

## 10:52–11:12 — Debrief and CTA

**STORY FUNCTION**  
Deliver the thesis after the evidence, then exit cleanly.

**VISUAL**  
HOST direct to camera. Behind the host, the four operational objects return: tally,
key, uniform, car. Each disappears as its role is named, leaving the raised barrier.

**HOST**  
> They did not find one unguarded hole in Auschwitz. Four prisoners understood four
> different parts of its routine—and made the SS see what it expected to see: a work
> detail, a mechanic, officers, and a staff car.
>
> If you want the next journey into the choices that changed history, subscribe to
> `[CHANNEL NAME]`.

**AUDIO**  
Restrained resolution, not victory music. Finish on the mechanical click of the raised
barrier locking into place.

## Caption policy

- Caption every host and narration line.
- Caption all decisive dialogue, including “Kazek, do something” and Piechowski's
  translated order.
- Keep captions below the primary face/action safe area and do not add a full-width
  dark backplate unless a shot fails contrast QA.
- Label the gate command on first appearance as `TRANSLATED RECONSTRUCTION` through a
  small source tag, not inside the spoken caption.

## Known uncertainty retained in the script

- Bendera's reported death warning is explicitly attributed to Piechowski.
- The exact German wording at the final gate is not claimed; the line is labeled a
  translated reconstruction.
- The exact location and cause of the car's final stop are not asserted.
- Jaster's death is described as unresolved.
- No chase, gunfire, dogs, or guard attack is invented.
