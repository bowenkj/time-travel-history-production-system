# EP01 Edited Storyline v1 — Four Prisoners, One Car, No Papers

Status: paper edit for review; not final narration
Target runtime: 10:45–11:15
Primary audience question: **How did four prisoners make the camp's own security
system carry them out?**

## Story promise

Four prisoners did not cut a fence or overpower a guard. They used a fake work detail,
copied keys, stolen SS uniforms, a staff car and one final performance of authority.
The episode shows how four separate contributions became one plan—and the moment the
plan nearly stopped at the final barrier.

## Presenter mode

- The host opens in a modern, direct-to-camera briefing and then appears inside the
  reconstructed historical environment as a field correspondent.
- The host can stand beside locations, demonstrate objects and explain invisible
  systems, but does not become a fifth member of the escape team.
- The four prisoners do not hold invented interviews with the host. Their character
  is revealed through documented choices, testimony-based dialogue and action.
- During the warehouse, car and final-barrier sequences, reduce host presence and let
  the four men own the screen.
- No standalone time-travel visual. The briefing's final sentence cuts directly into
  the first historical action.

## Beat map

### 1. 0:00–0:15 — Cold open: the barrier does not move

**Viewer question:** Why is the guard refusing to open the gate?
**Plot movement:** A Steyr 220 approaches the outer checkpoint. Four men in SS
uniforms sit inside. The guard does not reach for the barrier. Distance collapses:
80 meters, 60, 20. Bendera stops the car.
**Character ownership:** Bendera at the wheel; Piechowski in the senior uniform;
Jaster guarding something beneath his coat; Lempart watching the guard.
**Host/narration function:** Minimum orientation only: four prisoners, stolen car,
stolen uniforms, no valid papers.
**Visual plan:** Engine vibration; fingers on wheel; sweat at Bendera's temple; four
recognizable close-ups; barrier handle untouched; Piechowski's hand near the holster.
**Sound:** Engine, tires on gravel, distant guard call, music pulse narrowing toward
silence.
**Exit hook:** Lempart: “Kazek, do something.” Piechowski opens the door. Cut before
he speaks.

### 2. 0:15–0:21 — Brand ident

Six seconds maximum. Maintain the cold-open pulse. The final sound hit should land on
the host's first word, not release the tension.

### 3. 0:21–0:48 — Mission briefing: how did they get into that car?

**Viewer question:** What sequence of actions could possibly produce the image we just
saw?
**Plot movement:** Establish 20 June 1942, Auschwitz I, and one clean rule: clearing
the prisoner compound was not enough; an outer guarded boundary still required
permission. Four prisoners had to reach a warehouse, become convincing SS men, obtain
a car and cross that final line.
**Host function:** Face-to-camera with a simple physical diagram: inner compound → HWL
warehouse/garage → outer checkpoint. No general Holocaust history lecture.
**Visual plan:** Moving three-point map under the host; flash one image/object for each
dependency—key, uniform, car, barrier.
**Verbal launch:** “To understand how four prisoners ended up inside that car, we need
to go back to the moment a mechanic arrived with half a plan.”
**Transition:** Hard cut from “half a plan” to Bendera working under the hood of the
Steyr. No portal or travel montage.

### 4. 0:48–1:29 — Bendera must escape; Piechowski cannot accept the cost

**Viewer question:** Why attempt this now, and what is missing from Bendera's idea?
**Plot movement:** Introduce Bendera through work, not biography. Attribute to
Piechowski's later account that Bendera learned he was marked to be killed. He can
obtain a car, but Piechowski hesitates for a different reason: he remembers the SS
warning that an escape could bring lethal retaliation against the escapee's real work
group or block. One man's urgent need to flee collides with another man's refusal to
make uninvolved prisoners pay. His practical problem is just as severe: electrified
fences, an inner gate and an outer checkpoint.

**Host function:** Translate access into a modern, legible contradiction: possession
of the fastest car inside the camp is useless without a believable reason for every
guard to let it pass.
**Visual plan:** Bendera repairing the Steyr; a prisoner file closing; a restrained,
testimony-based reconstruction of Bendera bringing the warning to Piechowski; other
prisoners at roll call; guard lines overlaid on the geography; car blocked visually by
two barriers.
**Mini-payoff:** The plan will not attack the security system. It will imitate the
people allowed through it.
**Exit hook:** The way out of the moral and procedural trap is the same: they need a
group that does not officially exist.

### 5. 1:29–2:10 — Build a four-man team that does not exist on paper

**Viewer question:** Why these four men?
**Plot movement:** Piechowski and Bendera solve the first crisis by forming a fake
rubbish Kommando, then bring in Jaster and Lempart. Because it is not an existing work
group, it gives the four a routine-looking cover while reducing the chance that a real
Kommando will be blamed for losing them. Establish contributions in motion:

- Piechowski understands German and can report the detail;
- Jaster has a resistance connection and can help manufacture access;
- Lempart completes the four-man work cover and becomes the pressure point inside the
  car;
- Bendera has the mechanical route to the vehicle.

**Host function:** No full biographies. State that the plan only works if four roles
arrive at the same place on the same afternoon.
**Visual plan:** Four men converge separately on a worktable; four objects land in
sequence: prisoner tally, lock, insignia, car key.
**Open loop:** Briefly show Jaster receiving or concealing a folded report without yet
explaining it.
**Exit hook:** The SS uniforms are locked inside a building they cannot legally enter
after work ends.

### 6. 2:10–3:00 — To copy the keys, first break the locks

**Viewer question:** How can prisoners obtain keys to a secured SS repository?
**Plot movement:** Based on Bendera's testimony, Jaster deliberately damages three
locks. Bendera, the mechanic, is assigned to repair them and uses that authorized
contact to make bump keys. Piechowski prepares the coal hatch and rank insignia.

**Host function:** Demonstrate the causal chain in one short object-led explanation:
broken lock → repair assignment → key access.
**Visual plan:** Jaster's hand damaging a mechanism; Bendera examining the lock in
front of an SS supervisor; a key impression or improvised key taking shape; Piechowski
loosening the hatch bolt and sewing insignia.
**Mini-payoff:** They now have a quiet route through most of the building.
**Exit hook:** “Most” will become important when they reach the one door the keys
cannot open.

### 7. 3:00–3:48 — Saturday afternoon: the plan becomes irreversible

**Viewer question:** How do four prisoners leave the main compound without triggering
an alarm?
**Plot movement:** Saturday work ends around midday. The four hide in the attic of an
unfinished block, review the plan and wait for other Kommandos to disperse. They move
behind the kitchen, fill a trolley with rubbish and ash, and assume the role of a
routine work detail.
**Character ownership:** Lempart's later testimony supplies the physical details;
Piechowski takes the speaking position.
**Host function:** One sentence on the stakes, attributed to Piechowski: they believed
failure meant death and feared punishment for others. Do not linger on a farewell
speech.
**Visual plan:** Attic slats and light; four hands over a simple route sketch; ash
hitting the cart; guard tower visible behind the kitchen.
**Sound:** Work bell, departing footsteps, trolley wheel beginning its repeating
rhythm.
**Exit hook:** The cart reaches the inner gate. The guard asks for the detail.

### 8. 3:48–4:34 — The first gate opens—and reveals another prison

**Viewer question:** Will the guard check whether this four-man detail exists?
**Plot movement:** Piechowski gives his prisoner number and adds “three.” The guard
records the movement without investigating further. The four pass the inner camp gate
and walk toward HWL.
**Host function:** Deliver the geography reveal only after the apparent success: they
are outside the inner compound but still inside the large guard chain, watched by
towers and blocked by the final checkpoint.
**Visual plan:** Close-up of number being written; cart crossing under the gate;
camera widens to reveal the outer chain, towers and distant barrier.
**Mini-payoff:** The fake detail worked.
**Exit hook:** Their destination is now closed and empty. If anyone sees them entering,
there is no legitimate explanation.

### 9. 4:34–5:29 — The keys work until they do not

**Viewer question:** Can they enter, change and leave before staff return or an alarm
is raised?
**Plot movement:** They reach HWL, open the prepared coal hatch and enter the basement.
The bump keys carry them through the planned internal route. At the weapons-storage
door, the quiet plan stops working. They use a crowbar.
**Character ownership:** Bendera's preparation gets them inside; collective force gets
them through the final door.
**Host function:** Stay mostly out. One short line converts the setback into risk: a
forced door will expose the escape even if the men do not.
**Visual plan:** Exterior emptiness; coal dust; key turns; doors opening in succession;
then a key that refuses to turn; glances; crowbar bites into the frame.
**Sound:** Drop music during key attempts; amplify metal strain and the eventual crack.

**Exit hook:** Behind the door are the uniforms and guns—but putting them on creates a
new problem for the only man who can retrieve the car.

### 10. 5:29–6:20 — Three officers and one prisoner

**Viewer question:** How can Bendera reach the garage without exposing the disguise?
**Plot movement:** Piechowski, Jaster and Lempart change first and take weapons.
Piechowski wears the senior insignia. Bendera puts SS underclothes on but keeps his
prisoner blouse visible because guards know him as the prisoner mechanic who sometimes
moves vehicles.
**Character ownership:** Piechowski assembles the visual performance; Bendera chooses
to separate from the group and walk exposed toward the garage.
**Visual plan:** Avoid fetishized uniform montage. Focus on identity mechanics: striped
clothes off, rank tab sewn, helmet chinstraps, prisoner blouse covering the disguise.
Jaster checks the folded report remains concealed.
**Host function:** Explain the paradox in one sentence: dressed as an SS man, Bendera
would look suspicious at the garage; dressed as himself, he has one chance to look
routine.
**Exit hook:** He leaves the other three behind and crosses the open space alone.

### 11. 6:20–7:12 — Steal the car in plain sight

**Viewer question:** Will anyone stop a prisoner opening the SS garage?
**Plot movement:** Bendera crosses to the garage, opens it with a key and drives out the
Steyr 220. Because he has moved cars before on Kreutzmann's orders, the act itself does
not trigger alarm. He returns to HWL, finishes changing, and the four load the car.
Lempart sees approximately 3:45 p.m. on the main building clock.
**Host function:** Identify the car accurately as Kreutzmann's staff car, not repeat
the popular “Höss's commandant car” version. Keep the correction under five seconds.

**Visual plan:** Long exposed walk; garage key; engine ignition; car emerging; the
three disguised men waiting behind the side door; 3:45 clock insert.
**Mini-payoff:** All four dependencies finally occupy the same car.
**Exit hook:** The uniforms work immediately—SS men begin saluting them—but every
salute brings them closer to the checkpoint where papers may be demanded.

### 12. 7:12–7:57 — The disguise succeeds too early

**Viewer question:** Will repeated success make them look more convincing or make the
final failure worse?
**Plot movement:** The Steyr moves through the support area. SS personnel salute. The
four return the performance without stopping. For a few seconds, the plan appears to
be working perfectly.
**Character ownership:** Piechowski must remain visibly authoritative; the others must
not react like prisoners seeing guards salute them.
**Host function:** One line: there is still no evidence they possess the pass required
to cross the large guard chain.
**Visual plan:** Salutes reflected across car windows; Jaster's grip on the hidden
report; Lempart watching the approaching booth; barrier small in the distance.
**Sound:** Music rises but with unresolved low note; engine becomes the timing device.

**Exit hook:** The barrier does not move. Rejoin the cold open without repeating its
orientation narration.

### 13. 7:57–9:16 — “Kazek, do something”

**Viewer question:** Can Piechowski create authority before the guard starts asking
questions?
**Plot movement:** Reconstruct the approach from Piechowski's memory: 80 meters, 60,
20. Bendera's face loses color. The car stops. Piechowski freezes. Lempart strikes or
prompts him: “Kazek, do something.” Piechowski opens the door, shouts translated
military abuse, moves his hand to the holster and orders the guard to open the gate.
The guard runs to the handle, raises the barrier and salutes. Bendera drives through.

**Character ownership:** The climax belongs jointly to Lempart's prompt, Piechowski's
performance and Bendera's control of the car. Jaster carries the evidence through the
opening.
**Host function:** None once the countdown begins. Do not narrate over the decisive
line or the barrier movement.
**Visual plan:** Match cold-open shots, then extend them; guard's hand finally moves;
barrier rises; car passes close enough for faces to be seen; do not cut away before the
physical crossing is complete.
**Sound:** Reduce music during the stop; translated order dry and foregrounded;
mechanical barrier sound; music returns only after the rear of the car clears.
**Payoff:** The plan succeeds at the exact point where preparation runs out and human
performance takes over.
**Exit hook:** Do not celebrate yet. The checkpoint can raise an alarm, the roads can
be blocked, and the stolen car is immediately identifiable.

### 14. 9:16–10:04 — Outside the barrier is not the end

**Viewer question:** Can they become untraceable before the alarm spreads?
**Plot movement:** Crosscut the apparent freedom with the response inside Auschwitz.
Roll call identifies four missing men; an emergency SS search unit is dispatched and
an escape telegram begins moving to police and border posts. The men leave main roads
and use forest routes. Their greatest advantage then disappears: the car eventually
breaks down or is abandoned. Avoid asserting the disputed exact location. The four
continue on foot and separate. Lempart becomes ill according to Piechowski's account.

**Host function:** Return briefly as field correspondent beside the abandoned vehicle.
Explain that the Steyr solved the checkpoint, not the pursuit.
**Visual plan:** Fast road intercut with the empty roll-call spaces, duty ledger,
search unit mobilizing and telegram transmission; turn into forest; mechanical failure
without inventing a specific cause; uniforms discarded/covered; footprints splitting.
**Sound:** Music shifts from triumphant release back to pursuit pulse; distant alarm
or telegram keying begins.
**Exit hook:** One of the men is carrying something whose survival matters beyond the
four of them.

### 15. 10:04–10:52 — The fifth payload: a report from inside Auschwitz

**Viewer question:** What was Jaster carrying, and what did escape cost afterward?
**Plot movement:** Reveal that Jaster carried a report from Witold Pilecki intended for
Home Army headquarters. The Museum says he probably completed the mission. Briefly
resolve the fates:

- Lempart hid near his family's region and survived the war;
- Bendera survived using false papers and mechanical work;
- Piechowski joined the Home Army and survived the war;
- Jaster entered Home Army special operations and died in unresolved circumstances in
  1943;
- Jaster's parents were imprisoned at Auschwitz and both died.

**Host function:** Do not turn this into four mini-biographies. Use a single moving
map and one line per person. State uncertainty around Jaster's death.
**Visual plan:** Report in hand; underground courier route; four paths separating;
official escape telegram racing outward in the opposite direction.
**Payoff:** The plan carried both four people and testimony beyond the guard chain.

## Tension architecture

The episode should feel like six rising waves, not one continuous wall of loud music.
Each quiet passage is anticipatory pressure before a sharper conflict.

| Wave | Setup | Conflict / setback | Local climax | New danger created |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — motive | Bendera can obtain a car | He may be killed, but escape may bring retaliation on others | They invent a fake four-man Kommando | The group still lacks access to uniforms and weapons |
| 2 — access | Jaster creates a repair opportunity; Bendera makes keys | One critical door will not open | They force the weapons room with a crowbar | The broken door can expose the plan at any moment |
| 3 — first departure | Saturday leaves HWL empty; rubbish cart provides cover | Inner guard may check whether the detail exists | Piechowski's “number plus three” is accepted | They are still trapped inside the outer guard chain |
| 4 — transformation | Three men become SS personnel | Bendera cannot approach the garage dressed as SS | He uses his prisoner identity to retrieve the Steyr in plain sight | The four must now survive close-range scrutiny together |
| 5 — final barrier | SS personnel salute the car, creating a false victory | Outer guard does not raise the barrier; Piechowski freezes | Lempart prompts him; Piechowski forces obedience; barrier rises | The stolen car and four missing names can trigger a manhunt |
| 6 — escape aftershock | Forest roads create distance | Roll call triggers search and telegram; the Steyr fails | They abandon the car and split | Jaster must still deliver Pilecki's report; families remain exposed |

### Conflict variety

- **Moral conflict:** flee now versus risk punishment of others.
- **Procedural conflict:** a work detail, keys and passes must survive bureaucracy.
- **Physical conflict:** the weapons-room door refuses to open.
- **Identity conflict:** Bendera must look like a prisoner before looking like an SS
  man; Piechowski must perform rank he does not possess.
- **Interpersonal pressure:** Piechowski freezes; Lempart forces the decision.
- **Pursuit conflict:** roll call, emergency search, telegram, failed car.

Do not invent guard attacks, gunfire, dogs at the car or arguments among the four to
increase intensity. The documented plan already supplies varied conflict.

## Retention hard gates

- A legible life-or-death problem appears before 0:05.
- The main mission is clear before 0:35.
- No context-only passage lasts more than 15 seconds without an object, choice or
  action on screen.
- Every apparent success reveals an additional security layer or cost within 10
  seconds.
- At least three false victories occur: the inner gate accepts the detail, the keys
  open the internal route, and SS personnel salute the stolen car.
- Major intensity peaks occur around 2:45, 4:15, 5:15, 6:50, 8:45 and 9:40 rather than
  saving all energy for one ending.
- Music must leave dynamic room: use near-silence for the failed key, Bendera's exposed
  walk and the stopped car; reserve the strongest cue for the final barrier and search
  crosscut.
- The final factual payoff—the report—arrives only after the physical escape payoff,
  giving viewers a reason to remain after the gate opens.

### 16. 10:52–11:12 — Debrief and next-story handoff

**Grand payoff:** They escaped not because Auschwitz had one unguarded hole, but
because four prisoners understood four different parts of its routine and made the SS
see what it expected to see: a work detail, a mechanic, officers and a staff car.

**Host delivery:** Direct, restrained, under 20 seconds. No generic motivational moral.
If a next episode is selected, bridge with one concrete contrast: “This plan depended
on being seen. The next escape depended on disappearing.”
**CTA:** One sentence after the historical payoff, not before it.

## Character agency QA

- **Bendera:** originates operational design, creates keys, prepares and drives car.
- **Piechowski:** reports work detail, prepares insignia, carries senior disguise,
  performs at checkpoint.
- **Jaster:** helps manufacture key access, joins disguise, carries Pilecki report.
- **Lempart:** completes cover, supplies testimony-based viewpoint, triggers the
  decisive action at the barrier.

All four appear before 2:10, all four make an identifiable contribution, and the final
gate is not framed as a solo Piechowski victory.
